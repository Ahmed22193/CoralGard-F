Understanding Our Coral Reefs

Explore the different states of coral reefs and learn how we can protect these vital underwater ecosystems.

Coral Reef States

Healthy Coral

Vibrant and colorful, teeming with life. Healthy corals are resilient and form the foundation of diverse and thriving ecosystems.

Bleached Coral

Pale and stressed, a sign of distress often caused by rising ocean temperatures. Bleaching can be reversed if environmental conditions improve in time.

Dead Coral

White and brittle, covered in algae, and no longer supporting life. Dead reefs can erode, impacting coastal protection and the entire ecosystem.

Tips & Facts

Coral reefs support 25% of all marine life.

Protecting reefs helps maintain critical biodiversity.

Sustainable tourism is key to reef health.

Did You Know?

info

Coral reefs are often called the 'rainforests of the sea' because they support such a high diversity of life. They provide shelter, breeding grounds, and food for countless marine species. Protecting these vital ecosystems is crucial for the health of our oceans and the planet.
